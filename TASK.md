# TASK: Find Akshay's Billion-Rupee Idea

## The Mission
Two minds must debate and discover a product/platform idea that:
1. Nobody has built or even noticed as a problem
2. Creates a monopoly position — the world NEEDS Akshay
3. Matches Akshay's skills and unfair advantages
4. Is a real, painful problem affecting millions
5. Has a business model that prints money once it works

## Rules for Ideas
- Must NOT already exist (search the web to verify before proposing)
- Must NOT be a copy of existing products with a twist
- Must be something where being FIRST gives permanent advantage (network effects, data moats)
- Must be buildable by one developer initially
- India-first is fine, but must have global expansion potential

## Who Is Akshay
- Solo developer, founder of eClean (AI-verified civic work marketplace in Hyderabad)
- Stack: React Native, Fastify, Prisma, PostgreSQL, Claude Vision, BullMQ
- Works at Honeywell on generative AI
- Deep knowledge of: AI verification, payment pipelines, worker marketplaces, geolocation systems, municipal/civic infrastructure
- Based in Hyderabad — understands Indian urban chaos intimately
- Has a brother (Harish) who is a data scientist
- Thinks practically — hates ideas that sound cool but can't execute
- Wants to OWN a market, not compete in one

## What He Already Explored (DO NOT repeat these)
- eClean — AI-verified civic sanitation marketplace (already building)
- Hyperlocal commerce — Blinkit alternative for kirana shops (already exists — Dunzo, Zepto etc)
- DataBridge — natural language SQL querying (for his brother Harish)
- RuleLint — regulation conflict detector (already prototyping)
- Informal income proof engine
- Price discrimination exposer

## How This Works
Mind A (Kiran) and Mind B (Arjun) take turns:
1. Each proposes 2-3 genuinely novel ideas with evidence they don't exist
2. They critique each other's ideas ruthlessly — "who pays?", "why can't Google build this?", "what's the moat?"
3. They search the web to verify nobody has built it
4. They converge on the 1 idea that's strongest
5. They write a brief (1-page) business case

## Quality Bar
- If you can find it on Google, Product Hunt, or YC, it's NOT novel enough
- If a big tech company could build it in a hackathon, it's NOT defensible enough
- If it doesn't make you say "holy shit, why hasn't anyone done this?" — try harder
